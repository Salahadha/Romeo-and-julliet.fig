Figurative Language



what is Figurative Language ?

Figurative language is the use of words or expressions to convey meaning beyond
their literal interpretation. It adds depth, creativity, and emotional impact to writing by:
Painting vivid mental images Evoking strong
emotions Highlighting connections between ideas Making abstract concepts tangible

What Are The Figurative Language Used in Romeo & Juliet

1. Metaphor



Directly compares two unrelated things without "like" or "as."
Example: "Juliet is the sun" (Act 2, Scene 2). Romeo compares Juliet to the sun, implying she is his light and life.


2. Simile



Compares two things using "like" or "as."
Example: "Love goes toward love as schoolboys from their books" (Act 2, Scene 2). Love’s eagerness is likened to children escaping school.


3. Personification



Gives human traits to non-human
Example: "Death lies on her like an untimely frost" (Act 4, Scene 5). Death is portrayed as a physical force.


4. Hyperbole



Exaggeration for emphasis.
Example: "My bounty is as boundless as the sea, my love as deep" (Act 2, Scene 2). Juliet exaggerates to express infinite love.


5. Oxymoron



Pairs contradictory terms.
Example: "loving hate," "heavy lightness" (Act 1, Scene 1). Captures Romeo’s emotional turmoil.
